    Say \u2018hello\u2019 to Buddy the otter cub. The baby otter was found crying on a doorstep after becoming separated from his mother during severe weather in Dumfries and Galloway in Scotland.<\/p>\n

    \u201cThis is a very hazardous time for young otters as they can easily be washed out of their holts by high rivers and at this age they are not strong swimmers,\u201d explains the Scottish SPCA, who is caring for Buddy.<\/p>\n

    The 10-week-old cub was taken to the Scottish SPCA\u2019s National Wildlife Rescue Centre in Fishcross where he is getting fed milk and hand fed a diet of fish.<\/p>\n

    Buddy won\u2019t be all alone for long either. He will be introduced to three other orphaned otter cubs, Ebb, Tide and Wave.<\/p>\n

    <\/p>\n

    <\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

     <\/p>\n

    The humane society says, \u201cThey will stay with us for around a year until they are old enough to fend for themselves back in the wild.\u201d<\/p>\n

    I\u2019m so glad someone heard Buddy\u2019s cries for help. The little guy\u2019s story is a reminder to everyone that if you find an orphaned or hurt wild animal, please call your local SPCA or wildlife rehabilitation centre.<\/p>\n
