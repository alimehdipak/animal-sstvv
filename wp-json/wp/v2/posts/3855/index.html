    \u00a0A dog that had been missing for two months was rescued by a team of speleologists. Due to the layout of the building, their objective was especially challenging, as reported by positive-info.<\/p>\n

    Almost two months after disappearing on June 9, a dog named Abby was discovered and saved by cave divers on August 6.<\/p>\n

    \n
    \n
    \n
    <\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n
    \n

    When hunting for Abby, how did her family not notice that she had wandered off and ended up in a cave? Only she can provide a definitive response to this inquiry. Even though she had access to water and freshwater fish and crustaceans, it is unknown how she survived in this unfriendly and entirely dark habitat.<\/p>\n

    <\/p>\n

    However, experts immediately went into action to save the puppy after learning that she had been seen in the Tom Moore subterranean tunnels between Brewer and Perryville in Missouri. The crew of cavers included Rick Haley and Gerry Keene. They went straight into the 35 km long maze. They eventually tracked down the animal, but freeing it was no easy task.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

    \n

    They squished Abby into a 60-centimeter opening, then climbed the rock face 150 meters upward to the surface.<\/p>\n

    <\/p>\n

    Robert Cahoon, deputy chief of the Biehle fire brigade, helped them outside. Between the two of them, they canvassed the area looking for the dog\u2019s owners. Once they found the correct address, they were able to enter. Abby\u2019s owners, who had given up hope that they would ever see their dog alive again after so much time had passed since she had vanished, were shocked. Back at home, the dog is taking it easy as she recovers from her ordeal.<\/p>\n
