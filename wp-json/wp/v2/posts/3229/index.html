The dog became the pony\u2019s friend and protector after the death of his mother\u00a0<\/strong><\/p>\n

Dogs are such caring and affectionate animals. They have great protective instincts and will often be around an animal if they feel they need help.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

It is similar to the case of a rescue dog named Zip who became the orphan pony\u2019s best friend and guardian.<\/p>\n

Karla Swindle is the owner of S&K Quarter Horses in Fayette, Alabama. A few years ago her 22-year-old mare named Sandy \u2018di.ed\u2019 of declining health.<\/p>\n

The passing of her mare brings sadness to Sandy\u2019s foal, Tye, an orphan just nine days old.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

But an unexpected friend quickly appeared to comfort the orphaned pony: Zip\u2019s dog.<\/p>\n

Karla told The Dodo. \u201cZip spent the night next to the pony lying in the alley, and the dog looked after the horse like a father.\u201d<\/p>\n

Karla said that the bed was like a dog that sensed Tye\u2019s heartbreak, so it stayed with the pony all night, comforting him with a sympathetic paw.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

\u201cZip was lying there on the horse\u2019s neck and was groaning, like crying,\u201d Karla told the Lewiston Tribune, on IHeartDogs. \u201cIt just broke my heart.\u201d<\/p>\n

While Tye lost his mother, he gained a best friend: the dog quickly assumed the role of the orphan\u2019s friend and guardian.<\/p>\n

\u201cI think he adopted the horse as his son,\u201d Karla said. \u201cHe\u2019s letting the horse know\u201d it\u2019s not alone.\u00a0Dogs are very intelligent animals, emotionally seem to be able to sense the moment someone has \u2018pas.sed a.way\u2019, because of that Zip understood the situation and went to help the pony. this orphan.Karla told The Dodo: \u201cWe raise the ponies every year, and he would look in the door and just look at them. \u201cYou can tell Zip that something went wrong that night.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThis is the first time he\u2019s just licked, nudged, laid on one,\u201d she told the Tribune.Zip continued to take care of Tye daily for weeks and was eager to see his friend every morning.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Karla told The Dodo: \u201cEvery time I took off to the barn, Zip would run to the stall, stand in front of the stall and wait for me to get there. \u201cHe beat me up every time.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cAs soon as I opened the door, he would knock me out before I could get in there. If the pony is lying down, it will go there and lay its head on it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tye has since grown into a happy, healthy pony, no doubt thanks to Zip\u2019s care and friendship in the early days.<\/p>\n

Tye now spends most of his time with his fellow horses and, so to speak, he has to leave the nest, becoming more independent from his canine father.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

\u201cYou could say that when the pony needed Zip, Zip was there for it,\u201d says Karla. \u201cAnd now Zip knew the pony was fine, so they went their separate ways.\u201d<\/p>\n

Although they no longer rely on each other, we know these two will always be friends and have a special place in each other\u2019s hearts.<\/p>\n
